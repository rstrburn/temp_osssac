Records Section
How do I apply for an Official Transcript of Records, Diploma, and other documents?

  1. Visit PUP Online Document Request System (ODRS) through this link: https://odrs.pup.edu.ph/
  2. At the landing page, please select the campus you completed your studies.
  3. Once selected, please register a new account if this is your first time requesting for a document.
    If you have previously made a request through the ODRS, just log-in your account details.
  4. After logging in, click “New Request” redirecting you to the Document Requesting Form
  5. Fill out all the required fields and check all the documents you want to request a copy for.
  6. Click “Submit” to finalize the request.

How do I pay for the requested Official Transcript of Records, Diploma, and other documents?

The client has three options to pay for their requested documents: PUP over the counter payment, Landbank over the counter collection, or fund transfer through Landbank Online or Instapay.

PUP Cashier Over the counter

  1. In your ODRS account, please click “Payment Voucher” and print the same document as proof of payment.
  2. Visit the PUP Cashier located at the South Wing Ground Floor of the Main Building from 8:00AM to 5:00PM to pay for your request.

Note: Due to the ongoing coronavirus restrictions, the PUP Cashier has reduced working hours. Clients are encouraged to pay for their requested documents online for their own safety and convenience.

Landbank Over the Counter

  1. In your ODRS account, please click “Payment Voucher” and print the same document as proof of payment.
  2. Visit any Landbank of the Philippines (LBP) Branch near you to pay over the counter or directly deposit on the PUP Account as follows:
    • ACCOUNT NAME: PUP TRUST RECEIPTS
    • ACCOUNT NUMBER: 0682-1020-47
  3. After payment, please scan the validated deposit slip.
  4. Fill out the online PUP Payment Form (http://bit.ly/PUPPaymentForm) and upload a copy of the scanned validated deposit slip alongside the complete name of the client, program title, student ID number, breakdown of fees paid for, and LBP branch where you made the payment.
  5. Submit the form.
  6. Your payment will be reflected on your ODRS account at least 10 working days after you have entered your payment details.

Online Banking Services

  1. In your ODRS account, please click “Payment Voucher” and save a copy of the document as proof of payment.
  2. If you have an active Landbank iAccess account, you may use the LBP e-payment services to complete payment for your requested document. Alternatively, you may use the bank transfer services of other banks provided they allow transactions with Landbank. Please check these in the respective websites of your banks.
  3. In your account, click “Fund Transfer” and enter the account details of PUP and the exact amount of payment for your requested documents. Choose the “Instapay” option to record the payment in real-time, then click “Submit.”
  4. You may be required to enter a one-time password (OTP) before you can complete the transaction. The OTP is sent either through your registered mobile number or email address. Please enter the OTP in the designated box and click “Submit.”
  5. Once submitted, you will be provided with a receipt of fund transfer for reference. Screenshot this online receipt as proof of payment.
  6. Fill out the online PUP Payment Form (http://bit.ly/PUPPaymentForm) and upload a copy of the screenshot of the fund transfer receipt. The receipt should indicate the date and time of transaction for easy reference. Other details required in the online form include the complete name of the client, program title, student ID number, breakdown of fees paid for, and the transaction reference number.
  7. Submit the form.
  8. Your payment will be reflected on your ODRS account at least 10 working days after you have entered your payment details.

How do I claim the documents I applied for?

Admissions to PUP

  1. All admissions applications for new students are processed online through the PUP iApply.
  2. Applicants should meet the following requirements to take the entrance tests:
    1. Requirement 1
    2. Requirement 2
    3. Requirement N
  3. Students who seek to transfer to PUP may check these requirements (hyperlink).
  4. Students who seek re-admission to PUP may check these requirements (hyperlink).
  5. PUP also accepts entrance scholars for artists, campus journalists and athletes. You may visit this link (hyperlink) for further details about this scholarship.

Applying Online

  1. Before applying online, make sure you have already prepared a 2x2 inch colored photo with name tag, saved as JPEG (.jpg or .jpeg; file size should not be more than 300kb) in your computer, flash drive, or device.
  2. Go to PUP iApply for PUPCET page (http://www.pup.edu.ph/iapply/pupcet) and read all the information provided.
  3. Click “Apply Now.”
  4. Read the service agreement and click the “I have read…” button to begin your application.
  5. Click the “PUPCET” icon and answer the pre-application questionnaire to determine if you qualify for PUPCET. After this, click “Next.”
  6. Fill out the online form. Type and/or select the required information, then click “Next.” You will be redirected to a page where you need to verify all the information provided in the form.
    1. Make sure that all the information you have provided are correctly spelled. Your name and date of birth should be consistent with the information indicated in your PSA copy of birth certificate.
    2. Ensure that the email address provided is active and correct.
    3. Any misrepresentations (i.e. giving false/incorrect information) in your application will automatically invalidate your admission to the University.
  7. If the information provided has been duly reviewed and correct, read the service agreement and conform by checking the “Yes, I have read and understood…” box. Enter your complete name in the “Digital Signature” box and type the characters provided in the “Digital Security Code” to complete your application.
  8. Should you need to revise your application, click “Make Changes.” Otherwise, click “Next” to finally submit your application.
  9. After submission of your registration, you will receive your ePermit after five (5) working days.
  10. Please save your Reference Number.

Claiming your e-Permit

  1. Go to PUP iApply and click “Claim Permit.”
  2. Enter the required information and click “Submit.”
  3. After clicking “Submit,” you may encounter either of the following messages from the system:
    1. Application Successful - If you see this message, click “Download ePermit”. Click “Save” or “Open” when your browser asks you. This will download your ePermit. Print your ePermit in color.
    2. Problem with your Photo - If you see this message, the photo you uploaded did not meet the proper and required format (please read the Guidelines for ePermit Photo for more information). Scan a new photo with the correct format and click the “Upload Photo” button. This will upload your new photo to the system, which will be reviewed by an Admission Officer. Please allow three to five (3-5) workdays for photo validation. After 3-5 workdays, proceed to Step 1 of the Claim ePermit step.

Schedule of Entrance Tests

  1. Students who wish to study at PUP may take the College Entrance Tests (PUPCET) administered in four batches from October to February for the Sta. Mesa Campus, Institute of Technology and Open University – Undergraduate Program.
  2. For those who wish to study in PUP satellite branches and campuses, you may directly inquire at the respective registrars of your prospective branch or campus for the schedule of their PUPCET.
  3. Alternatively, you may visit https://www.pup.edu.ph/iapply/pupcet for a complete schedule of entrance examinations in the Sta. Mesa Campus and other satellite branches and campuses of PUP.

Enrolling in PUP

  1. PUPCET passers would be given an advisory regarding the date of Online Confirmation.
  2. After confirmation, prospective students would be given a separate date and time of their Enrolment.
  3. A student is considered officially enrolled when your registration certificate has been properly validated by the Office of Admission and Registration Services Section. Only students who are officially enrolled are allowed to attend classes.

Transferring to PUP
Transferring within the PUP System

  1. A student seeking transfer from a PUP branch/campus to another branch/campus within the PUP System may be admitted depending on the availability of slots.
  2. The student must:
    1. have a recommendation from the Director of the branch where he/she came from;
    2. have completed at least two (2) semesters;
    3. have no failing grade, dropped and withdrawn mark in any academic subject;
    4. have met the college academic course/program requirements; and
    5. have submitted the following requirement to the Admission Services:
      1. Transfer Credential/Honorable Dismissal
      2. Certification of Grades/Transcript of Records for evaluation purposes
      3. Receipt of Admission Fee payment
      4. PSA Copy of Birth Certificate
      5. Receipt of Admission fee payment, and
      6. Curriculum sheet

Transferring from another School

  1. A student seeking transfer from another school or university to PUP may be admitted, subject to the availability of slots and upon approval of the University President or his duly authorized representative.
  2. The student must:
    1. have passed the psychological test;
    2. have completed two (2) semesters or one (1) year (equivalent to 36 units only) for the last two (2) years in any four- or five-year program/degree/course in the school/university where he/she came from;
    3. have a weighted average of 2.0 or better with no failed, dropped or withdrawn subjects
    4. have met the college academic program/course requirements;
    5. have submitted the following requirements:
      1. Honorable Dismissal/Transfer Credentials
      2. Transcript of Records
      3. Certification of good moral character with school/university dry seal
      4. Course/Subject Description taken from other school/university
      5. PSA Copy of Birth Certificate
      6. Medical Clearance from the University Clinic
      7. Two (2) pcs. 2x2 picture with name tag
      8. Receipt of Admission fee payment, and
      9. Curriculum sheet
  3. Accreditation of Subjects - The subjects taken by a transferee from another school, which may be considered as reasonable equivalents of subjects in the University curriculum shall be given credit, subject to validation and approval by the Dean/Director concerned, and the University Registrar.
  4. Admission Certificate - The transferring student will be issued by the Admission Services the corresponding admission certificate to be used for enrollment purposes.

Re-Admission to PUP

  1. Students whose studies in PUP were discontinued for a period of time may be considered for re-admission depending on their previous academic performance and availability of slots.
  2. The returning student should pay the re-admission fee amounting to P 25.00 and secure a copy of his/her Transcript of Records and informative copy of grades two (2) months prior to the enrolment period.
  3. Fill out the Re-Admission Application Form from the Admission Services and present the following documents:
    1. Transcript of Records;
    2. Two (2) pieces 2x2 inch picture;
    3. One (1) piece 1x1 inch picture (with college background if you stopped for more than two (2) years);
    4. Latest Registration Certificates;
    5. Receipt of payment for re-admission fee; and,
    6. Curriculum Sheet
  4. Secure Clearance from the Accounting Office, University Medical Office and Academic Evaluation from the college by presenting the properly filled out form for re-admission and your Transcript of Records/Informative Copy of Grades.
  5. Submit the duly filled out Re-admission Form with the attached clearances from the offices concerned to the Admissions Services.
  6. The returning student will be issued a Re-admission Certificate by the ARO after completion of all requirements and if the application has been approved.
  7. If re-admitted within two (2) years, the returning student shall be allowed to continue their old curriculum; otherwise, they will continue under the new curriculum existing at the time of their re-admission.
  8. Proceed to the College of your chosen course and present the Admission Certificate for your enrollment and the photo copy of the required credentials.

Office of Student Services
Scholarship and Financial Assistance Services
Available Scholarship and Financial Assistance Programs

  1. Available scholarship programs and financial assistance are regularly released and updated in the Facebook Page of the Scholarship and Financial Assistance Services – PUP Main Campus. You may visit this website for prospective scholarships.
  2. The PUP website under the “Student” option also lists all the sponsors and benefactors of PUP for the past and current academic years.
  3. Under the Scholarship and Financial Assistance Services (SFAS) Office, the following scholarships are offered and processed: resident scholarship, special grants, entrance scholarship and student assistantship program.

Applying for PUP Resident Scholarship

  1. Interested applicants must have a GWA of at least 1.5 for President’s Listers while Dean’s Listers must have GWA of at least 1.75.
  2. No grade shall be below 2.5 and no INC, F, D grade.
  3. The top 10% in the ranking of President’s Listers will receive P1,000.00 and all resident scholars will receive certificates signed by the president.

Applying for Entrance Scholarships

  1. Entrance scholarships are awarded to senior high school graduates who did not pass the PUPCET but qualified because they graduated with high honors, and those who are certified student athletes, creative media artists, campus journalists and cultural artists.
  2. They are required to submit the following documents to avail of this scholarship:
    1. Requirement 1
    2. Requirement 2
    3. Requirement N

Applying for Student Assistantship Program

  1. Interested applicants must have at least one-year residency, with passing grades in the last enrolled semester.
  2. They must also pass the psychological exam and should be endorsed by the requesting office.
  3. They are required to submit the following documents to avail of this scholarship:
    1. Requirement 1
    2. Requirement 2
    3. Requirement N

Applying for Other Scholarships or Financial Assistance

  1. Students are allowed to receive only one (1) scholarship benefit or financial assistance.
  2. When applying for scholarship or financial assistance, the student must be presently enrolled and must prepare copies of the following documents:
    1. Copy of of registration card;
    2. Copy of grades;
    3. Good moral certificate; and,
    4. Proof of annual income of the family
  3. Student applicants must have must have a general weighted average and grade per subject of 2.25 or better and without a grade of INC, D or Dropped, W or Withdrawn and F or Failed.
  4. Some scholarship grantors are strict in evaluating the estimated annual income. For students who intend to avail for scholarship, the best estimate is P300,000.00 per year.
  5. Grantors have various forms of releasing allowances. Some release it personally while others let the University process the release of allowances through Landbank ATM.

Requesting for a Medical Clearance or Certificate

  1. Go to the PUP Website and click “Downloadable Forms” for “Students”.
  2. Download “Health Information Sheet for Students”.
  3. Fill out the form and do not leave questions unanswered.
  4. Email the duly accomplished form to medical@pup.edu.ph on your date of enrollment with either of the following subjects:
    1. For incoming PUP students: “Freshmen Student for Medical Clearance / Certificate”
    2. For transferees to PUP: “Transferring Student for Medical Clearance / Certificate”
    3. For students seeking re-admission: “Returning Student for Medical Clearance / Certificate” (Note: this is only applicable for students whose reasons for stopping is NOT due to any ailment, sickness, or medical condition).
  5. For students whose reasons of stopping is due to any ailment, sickness, or medical condition, complete the same Health Information Sheet Form, and email these with the same subject (“Returning Student for Medical Clearance / Certificate”) alongside the medical certificate from your physician indicating you are “Fit to return to school” and other medical recommendations, if applicable.
  6. Medical certificates will be sent to your emails within two (2) days upon submission. Otherwise, you may call our landline for follow-up and verification.
  7. For satellite branches and campuses, kindly contact your medical clinic for further instructions.
  8. Due to the coronavirus restrictions, examination and chest x-ray and other requirements if needed are temporarily deferred until further notice.

Library Services
Ninoy Aquino Library and Learning Resource Center
General Library Services

  1. The Ninoy Aquino Library and Learning Resource Center (NALLRC) is the main library and academic repository of PUP. The Library and Archives Sections are located at the 3rd Floor of the NALLRC Building, within the Sta. Mesa Campus premises.
  2. The Library is open from 7:00AM to 4:00PM given the limitations caused by the coronavirus pandemic. It has also limited face-to-face transactions as of the moment.
  3. Given these restrictions, only PUP faculty, staff, and students may borrow books. Outside researchers and non-PUP clients have yet to be accommodated.
  4. To obtain a Library Account, please log in to www.ils.pup.edu.ph. The attending librarian will give you a username and password. You may download e-resources such as e-books, journals, and databases on this website.
  5. The Library does not sell books.

Borrowing Books

  1. Books and other reference materials may be requested to be borrowed online and checked out of the NALLRC with minimal contact. Under the initial phase, only PUP faculty, staff, and students can request items for checkout.
  2. To place a request, search for library materials in the library Online Public Access Catalog (OPAC). Use your PUP Library Account for this. You may request up to five (5) items for faculty, administrative employees, officials; and three (3) items for students at one time.
  3. When your items are ready, you will receive an email sent to your PUP webmail address directing you to use an online calendar to schedule a checkout date and time and provide you with details about how to get your items.
  4. Your books will already be checked out for you and placed in a paper bag, minimizing contact. Each library location will have a takeout approach that involves minimal contact between you and the library staff.
  5. Bring your PUP ID card on your scheduled checkout appointment and follow safety and health measures.
